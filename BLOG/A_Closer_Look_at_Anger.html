In the intricate tapestry of human emotions, happiness and sadness delicately sit within the recesses of our minds.
They're the cherished children of our internal world, cradled by tears and smiles, held close to our hearts as favorites,
the cared-for fragments of our emotional spectrum.

In stark contrast, anger is a formidable force that refuses to be confined to the depths of our consciousness.
It's not a fleeting visitor like happiness or sadness; instead,
it lingers in the palms of our hands, balled up like fists ready to shatter the fragile equilibrium of our existence.
It breaks bones and glass, leaving behind a trail of destruction, a testament to the intensity with which it grips our very being.

Happy and sad emotions find expression in the subtleties of our faces, leaving an indelible mark on our countenance.
The dried tears and bright smiles become a spectacle for passersby, inviting stares and curious glances.
They are frozen moments, snapshots of our emotional landscape, captured in the still image of memory.

Anger, on the other hand, refuses to be frozen. It is a constant, an ever-present companion that permeates every waking moment of our lives.
It manifests as a glass plate thrown against a wall, as obscenities screamed at a mirror,
as the violent act of ripping hair from our heads in a futile attempt to rid ourselves of the pain that simmers within.
The aftermath is bloodied hands and torn curtains, physical manifestations of the internal turmoil we're unwilling to share with the world.

In the realm of emotions, anger is the ugly that we often keep hidden, a darkness that we're afraid to expose to the light.
It's an internal struggle, a battle between the desire to release the pent-up rage and the fear of facing the consequences of that release.
The more we try to suppress it, the more it festers, consuming our faces and chests, screaming to be acknowledged.

The dichotomy between the ephemeral beauty of happiness and the enduring ugliness of anger is a constant tension within ourselves.
While the former is a fleeting visitor, a cherished moment frozen in time, the latter is an ever-present force that demands acknowledgment.
It's a struggle that defines our emotional landscape, a journey toward understanding and acceptance of the complexities that reside within us.

As we navigate the labyrinth of our emotions, it becomes imperative to recognize that anger, too, is a part of the human experience.
It's not a flaw but a facet of our intricate selves. By acknowledging and addressing the darkness within,
we can begin the transformative process of finding balance and embracing the full spectrum of our emotions.
In doing so, we unravel the tapestry, revealing the nuanced interplay between happiness, sadness, and the often misunderstood emotion that is anger.
